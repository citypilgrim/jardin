1.  As the deer pants for streams of water,so my soul longs after You, O God.  For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
2.  My soul thirsts for God, the living God.When shall I come and appear in God’s presence?  For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
3.  My tears have been my foodboth day and night,while men ask me all day long,“Where is your God?”  For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
4.  These things come to mind as I pour out my soul:how I walked with the multitude,leading the procession to the house of Godwith shouts of joy and praise.   For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
5.  Why are you downcast, O my soul?Why the unease within me?Put your hope in God, for I will yet praise Himfor the salvation of His presence.   For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
6.  O my God, my soul despairs within me.Therefore I remember Youfrom the land of Jordan and the peaks of Hermon—even from Mount Mizar.  For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
7.  Deep calls to deepin the roar of Your waterfalls;all Your breakers and waveshave rolled over me.  For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
8.  The LORD decrees His loving devotion by day,and at night His song is with meas a prayer to the God of my life.   For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
9.  I say to God my Rock,“Why have You forgotten me?Why must I walk in sorrowbecause of the enemy’s oppression?”  For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
10. Like the crushing of my bones,my enemies taunt me,while they say to me all day long,“Where is your God?”   For the choirmaster. A Maskil of the sons of Korah.
11. Why are you downcast, O my soul?Why the unease within me?Put your hope in God, for I will yet praise Him,my Savior and my God.
