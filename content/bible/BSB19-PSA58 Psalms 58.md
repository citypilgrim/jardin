1.  Do you indeed speak justly, O rulers?Do you judge uprightly, O sons of men?For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
2.  No, in your hearts you devise injustice;with your hands you mete out violence on the earth. For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
3.  The wicked are estranged from the womb;the liars go astray from birth.For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
4.  Their venom is like the venom of a snake,like a cobra that shuts its ears,For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
5.  refusing to hear the tune of the charmerwho skillfully weaves his spell. For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
6.  O God, shatter their teeth in their mouths;O LORD, tear out the fangs of the lions.For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
7.  May they vanishlike water that runs off;when they draw the bow,may their arrows be blunted.For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
8.  Like a slug that dissolves in its slime,like a woman’s stillborn child,may they never see the sun. For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
9.  Before your pots can feel the burning thorns—whether green or dry—He will sweep them away.For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
10. The righteous will rejoicewhen they see they are avenged;they will wash their feetin the blood of the wicked.For the choirmaster. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A Miktam of David.
11. Then men will say,“There is surely a reward for the righteous!There is surely a God who judges the earth!”
