1.  Give ear, O my people, to my instruction;listen to the words of my mouth.  A Maskil of Asaph.
2.  I will open my mouth in parables;I will utter things hidden from the beginning, A Maskil of Asaph.
3.  that we have heard and knownand our fathers have relayed to us. A Maskil of Asaph.
4.  We will not hide them from their children,but will declare to the next generationthe praises of the LORD and His might,and the wonders He has performed.  A Maskil of Asaph.
5.  For He established a testimony in Jacoband appointed a law in Israel,which He commanded our fathersto teach to their children, A Maskil of Asaph.
6.  that the coming generation would know them—even children yet to be born—to arise and tell their own children A Maskil of Asaph.
7.  that they should put their confidence in God,not forgetting His works,but keeping His commandments. A Maskil of Asaph.
8.  Then they will not be like their fathers,a stubborn and rebellious generation,whose heart was not loyal,whose spirit was not faithful to God.  A Maskil of Asaph.
9.  The archers of Ephraimturned back on the day of battle. A Maskil of Asaph.
10. They failed to keep God’s covenantand refused to live by His law. A Maskil of Asaph.
11. They forgot what He had done,the wonders He had shown them. A Maskil of Asaph.
12. He worked wonders before their fathersin the land of Egypt, in the region of Zoan. A Maskil of Asaph.
13. He split the sea and brought them through;He set the waters upright like a wall. A Maskil of Asaph.
14. He led them with a cloud by dayand with a light of fire all night. A Maskil of Asaph.
15. He split the rocks in the wildernessand gave them drink as abundant as the seas. A Maskil of Asaph.
16. He brought streams from the stoneand made water flow down like rivers.  A Maskil of Asaph.
17. But they continued to sin against Him,rebelling in the desert against the Most High. A Maskil of Asaph.
18. They willfully tested Godby demanding the food they craved. A Maskil of Asaph.
19. They spoke against God, saying,“Can God really prepare a table in the wilderness? A Maskil of Asaph.
20. When He struck the rock, water gushed outand torrents raged.But can He also give breador supply His people with meat?”  A Maskil of Asaph.
21. Therefore the LORD heardand was filled with wrath;so a fire was kindled against Jacob,and His anger flared against Israel, A Maskil of Asaph.
22. because they did not believe Godor rely on His salvation.  A Maskil of Asaph.
23. Yet He commanded the clouds aboveand opened the doors of the heavens. A Maskil of Asaph.
24. He rained down manna for them to eat;He gave them grain from heaven. A Maskil of Asaph.
25. Man ate the bread of angels;He sent them food in abundance. A Maskil of Asaph.
26. He stirred the east wind from the heavensand drove the south wind by His might. A Maskil of Asaph.
27. He rained meat on them like dust,and winged birds like the sand of the sea. A Maskil of Asaph.
28. He felled them in the midst of their camp,all around their dwellings. A Maskil of Asaph.
29. So they ate and were well filled,for He gave them what they craved.  A Maskil of Asaph.
30. Yet before they had filled their desire,with the food still in their mouths, A Maskil of Asaph.
31. God’s anger flared against them,and He put to death their strongestand subdued the young men of Israel.  A Maskil of Asaph.
32. In spite of all this, they kept on sinning;despite His wonderful works, they did not believe. A Maskil of Asaph.
33. So He ended their days in futility,and their years in sudden terror. A Maskil of Asaph.
34. When He slew them, they would seek Him;they repented and searched for God. A Maskil of Asaph.
35. And they remembered that God was their Rock,that God Most High was their Redeemer.  A Maskil of Asaph.
36. But they deceived Him with their mouths,and lied to Him with their tongues. A Maskil of Asaph.
37. Their hearts were disloyal to Him,and they were unfaithful to His covenant.  A Maskil of Asaph.
38. And yet He was compassionate;He forgave their iniquity and did not destroy them.He often restrained His angerand did not unleash His full wrath. A Maskil of Asaph.
39. He remembered that they were but flesh,a passing breeze that does not return.  A Maskil of Asaph.
40. How often they disobeyed Him in the wildernessand grieved Him in the desert! A Maskil of Asaph.
41. Again and again they tested Godand provoked the Holy One of Israel. A Maskil of Asaph.
42. They did not remember His power—the day He redeemed them from the adversary, A Maskil of Asaph.
43. when He performed His signs in Egyptand His wonders in the fields of Zoan.  A Maskil of Asaph.
44. He turned their rivers to blood,and from their streams they could not drink. A Maskil of Asaph.
45. He sent swarms of flies that devoured them,and frogs that devastated them. A Maskil of Asaph.
46. He gave their crops to the grasshopper,the fruit of their labor to the locust. A Maskil of Asaph.
47. He killed their vines with hailstonesand their sycamore-figs with sleet. A Maskil of Asaph.
48. He abandoned their cattle to the hailand their livestock to bolts of lightning.  A Maskil of Asaph.
49. He unleashed His fury against them,wrath, indignation, and calamity—a band of destroying angels. A Maskil of Asaph.
50. He cleared a path for His anger;He did not spare them from deathbut delivered their lives to the plague. A Maskil of Asaph.
51. He struck all the firstborn of Egypt,the virility in the tents of Ham.  A Maskil of Asaph.
52. He led out His people like sheepand guided them like a flock in the wilderness. A Maskil of Asaph.
53. He led them safely, so they did not fear,but the sea engulfed their enemies. A Maskil of Asaph.
54. He brought them to His holy land,to the mountain His right hand had acquired. A Maskil of Asaph.
55. He drove out nations before themand apportioned their inheritance;He settled the tribes of Israel in their tents.  A Maskil of Asaph.
56. But they tested and disobeyed God Most High,for they did not keep His decrees. A Maskil of Asaph.
57. They turned back and were faithless like their fathers,twisted like a faulty bow. A Maskil of Asaph.
58. They enraged Him with their high placesand provoked His jealousy with their idols.  A Maskil of Asaph.
59. On hearing it, God was furiousand rejected Israel completely. A Maskil of Asaph.
60. He abandoned the tabernacle of Shiloh,the tent He had pitched among men. A Maskil of Asaph.
61. He delivered His strength to captivity,and His splendor to the hand of the adversary. A Maskil of Asaph.
62. He surrendered His people to the swordbecause He was enraged by His heritage. A Maskil of Asaph.
63. Fire consumed His young men,and their maidens were left without wedding songs. A Maskil of Asaph.
64. His priests fell by the sword,but their widows could not lament.  A Maskil of Asaph.
65. Then the Lord awoke as from sleep,like a mighty warrior overcome by wine. A Maskil of Asaph.
66. He beat back His foes;He put them to everlasting shame. A Maskil of Asaph.
67. He rejected the tent of Josephand refused the tribe of Ephraim. A Maskil of Asaph.
68. But He chose the tribe of Judah,Mount Zion, which He loved. A Maskil of Asaph.
69. He built His sanctuary like the heights,like the earth He has established forever.  A Maskil of Asaph.
70. He chose David His servantand took him from the sheepfolds; A Maskil of Asaph.
71. from tending the ewes He brought himto be shepherd of His people Jacob,of Israel His inheritance. A Maskil of Asaph.
72. So David shepherded them with integrity of heartand guided them with skillful hands.
