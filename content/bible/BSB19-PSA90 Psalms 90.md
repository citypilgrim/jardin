1.  Lord, You have been our dwelling placethrough all generations.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
2.  Before the mountains were bornor You brought forth the earth and the world,from everlasting to everlastingYou are God.   A prayer of Moses the man of God.
3.  You return man to dust,saying, “Return, O sons of mortals.”  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
4.  For in Your sight a thousand yearsare but a day that passes,or a watch of the night.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
5.  You whisk them away in their sleep;they are like the new grass of the morning—  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
6.  in the morning it springs up new,but by evening it fades and withers.   A prayer of Moses the man of God.
7.  For we are consumed by Your angerand terrified by Your wrath.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
8.  You have set our iniquities before You,our secret sins in the light of Your presence.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
9.  For all our days decline in Your fury;we finish our years with a sigh.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
10. The length of our days is seventy years—or eighty if we are strong—yet their pride is but labor and sorrow,for they quickly pass, and we fly away.   A prayer of Moses the man of God.
11. Who knows the power of Your anger?Your wrath matches the fear You are due.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
12. So teach us to number our days,that we may present a heart of wisdom.   A prayer of Moses the man of God.
13. Return, O LORD! How long will it be?Have compassion on Your servants.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
14. Satisfy us in the morning with Your loving devotion,that we may sing for joy and be glad all our days.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
15. Make us glad for as many days as You have afflicted us,for as many years as we have seen evil.   A prayer of Moses the man of God.
16. May Your work be shown to Your servants,and Your splendor to their children.  A prayer of Moses the man of God.
17. May the favor of the Lord our God rest upon us;establish for us the work of our hands—yes, establish the work of our hands!
