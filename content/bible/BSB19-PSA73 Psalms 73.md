1.  Surely God is good to Israel,to those who are pure in heart.  A Psalm of Asaph.
2.  But as for me, my feet had almost stumbled;my steps had nearly slipped.  A Psalm of Asaph.
3.  For I envied the arrogantwhen I saw the prosperity of the wicked.   A Psalm of Asaph.
4.  They have no struggle in their death;their bodies are well-fed.  A Psalm of Asaph.
5.  They are free of the burdens others carry;they are not afflicted like other men.  A Psalm of Asaph.
6.  Therefore pride is their necklace;a garment of violence covers them.  A Psalm of Asaph.
7.  From their prosperity proceeds iniquity;the imaginations of their hearts run wild.  A Psalm of Asaph.
8.  They mock and speak with malice;with arrogance they threaten oppression.  A Psalm of Asaph.
9.  They set their mouths against the heavens,and their tongues strut across the earth.   A Psalm of Asaph.
10. So their people return to this placeand drink up waters in abundance.  A Psalm of Asaph.
11. The wicked say, “How can God know?Does the Most High have knowledge?”  A Psalm of Asaph.
12. Behold, these are the wicked—always carefree as they increase their wealth.   A Psalm of Asaph.
13. Surely in vain I have kept my heart pure;in innocence I have washed my hands.  A Psalm of Asaph.
14. For I am afflicted all day longand punished every morning.  A Psalm of Asaph.
15. If I had said, “I will speak this way,”then I would have betrayed Your children.  A Psalm of Asaph.
16. When I tried to understand all this,it was troublesome in my sight  A Psalm of Asaph.
17. until I entered God’s sanctuary;then I discerned their end.   A Psalm of Asaph.
18. Surely You set them on slick ground;You cast them down into ruin.  A Psalm of Asaph.
19. How suddenly they are laid waste,completely swept away by terrors!  A Psalm of Asaph.
20. Like one waking from a dream,so You, O Lord, awaken and despise their form.   A Psalm of Asaph.
21. When my heart was grievedand I was pierced within,  A Psalm of Asaph.
22. I was senseless and ignorant;I was a brute beast before You.   A Psalm of Asaph.
23. Yet I am always with You;You hold my right hand.  A Psalm of Asaph.
24. You guide me with Your counsel,and later receive me in glory.  A Psalm of Asaph.
25. Whom have I in heaven but You?And on earth I desire no one besides You.  A Psalm of Asaph.
26. My flesh and my heart may fail,but God is the strength of my heartand my portion forever.   A Psalm of Asaph.
27. Those far from You will surely perish;You destroy all who are unfaithful to You.  A Psalm of Asaph.
28. But as for me, it is good to draw near to God.I have made the Lord GOD my refuge,that I may proclaim all Your works.
